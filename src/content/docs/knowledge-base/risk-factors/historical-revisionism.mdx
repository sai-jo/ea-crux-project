## The Scenario

By 2030, AI can generate convincing historical "evidence"—documents, photographs, video, audio—that never existed. Holocaust denial gets "photographic evidence." Nationalist movements get "historical documents." Political figures get "archival footage" exonerating or condemning them.

**The threat isn't just fake evidence for the future—it's fake evidence for the past.** If we can't trust historical records, we lose the ability to learn from history, hold people accountable, or maintain shared understanding of what happened.

## Attack Vectors

### Vector 1: Denial Campaigns

**Mechanism**:
1. Genocide, atrocity, or crime occurred
2. AI generates "evidence" that it didn't (fake photos of different events)
3. Deniers cite synthetic "historical evidence"
4. Doubt introduced into established history

**At risk**:
- Holocaust documentation
- Armenian Genocide records
- Slavery historical evidence
- Colonial atrocities
- War crimes documentation

### Vector 2: Nationalist Mythology

**Mechanism**:
1. Political movement wants historical justification
2. AI generates "ancient documents," "archaeological evidence"
3. Territorial claims, ethnic superiority, historical grievances "documented"
4. Fake history drives real conflict

**At risk**:
- Territorial disputes
- Ethnic conflict justifications
- Origin stories
- Historical borders

### Vector 3: Personal Exoneration/Condemnation

**Mechanism**:
1. Historical figure's reputation at stake
2. AI generates "newly discovered" evidence
3. Exonerating or condemning evidence appears
4. Reputations rehabilitated or destroyed

**At risk**:
- Political legacies
- Criminal accountability
- Family histories
- Institutional histories

### Vector 4: Corporate/Institutional Records

**Mechanism**:
1. Company or institution has liability for past actions
2. AI generates "historical documents" showing different actions
3. Legal and moral accountability evaded

**At risk**:
- Environmental damage records
- Employment discrimination history
- Product safety documentation
- Financial records

## Why Historical Evidence Is Especially Vulnerable

### Limited Verification

| Factor | Vulnerability |
|--------|---------------|
| **Originals often lost** | Can't compare to authentic version |
| **Witnesses deceased** | Can't corroborate |
| **Context forgotten** | Don't know what to expect |
| **Archives incomplete** | Gaps can be filled with fakes |

### Trust in Age

| Factor | Vulnerability |
|--------|---------------|
| **"Old = authentic" assumption** | We trust aged documents |
| **Difficult to fake (historically)** | Lower suspicion |
| **Expertise rare** | Few people can evaluate |
| **Emotional weight** | Historical evidence feels authoritative |

### Limited Detection

| Factor | Vulnerability |
|--------|---------------|
| **Period materials unknown** | Don't know what paper/ink was used |
| **Style variation** | Historical styles varied more |
| **Technology differences** | Old photos have expected artifacts |
| **Archive access** | Difficult to check against collections |

## Defenses

### Technical

| Approach | Description | Limitation |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Digital archive verification** | Cryptographic signing of archives | Retrospective only |
| **Provenance chains** | Document chain of custody | Doesn't cover old materials |
| **Detection AI** | Analyze for synthetic artifacts | Arms race |
| **Material analysis** | Physical testing of documents | Destroys some evidence |

### Institutional

| Approach | Description | Limitation |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Archive integrity** | Secured, verified collections | Resources |
| **Expert networks** | Historians authenticate | Limited capacity |
| **Cross-reference systems** | Multiple independent records | Gaps in coverage |
| **Oral history preservation** | Record while witnesses live | Time-limited |

### Legal

| Approach | Description | Limitation |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Evidence standards** | Stricter historical evidence requirements | Burden of proof shifts |
| **Fabrication penalties** | Criminal liability for fake history | Jurisdiction |
| **Protected archives** | Legal protection for historical records | Enforcement |

## Why Separate Risk Factors from Risks?

**Risks** are harmful end states we want to avoid (loss of human agency, authoritarian takeover, etc.).

**Risk factors** are dynamics that make those end states more likely. This distinction matters because:

1. **Different interventions** - Addressing a risk factor (like racing dynamics) may prevent multiple downstream risks
2. **Clearer analysis** - Separating causes from effects helps identify leverage points
3. **Avoiding double-counting** - A risk factor isn't itself a harm, though it increases harm probability

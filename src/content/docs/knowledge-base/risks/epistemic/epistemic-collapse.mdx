## Overview

Epistemic collapse represents a **catastrophic breakdown** in society's collective ability to distinguish truth from falsehood, leading to the inability to form shared beliefs about fundamental aspects of reality. Unlike historical misinformation, this phenomenon is characterized by the complete erosion of reliable mechanisms for establishing factual consensus.

> **For comprehensive analysis**, see [Epistemic Health](/knowledge-base/parameters/epistemic-health/), which covers:
> - Current epistemic capacity levels and measurement
> - AI-driven threats to truth-seeking mechanisms
> - Factors that strengthen collective sense-making
> - Institutional responses and their effectiveness
> - Trajectory scenarios through 2030

## Key Mechanism

The collapse pathway:
1. **AI content floods channels** at scale beyond verification capacity
2. **Signal-to-noise degrades** as synthetic content overwhelms authentic
3. **Trust in institutions erodes** as verification fails
4. **Liar's dividend exploited** — all evidence becomes questionable
5. **Epistemic tribalization** — people retreat to identity-based truth
6. **Democratic deliberation fails** — no shared facts for decisions
7. **Coordinated action impossible** — can't agree on problems or solutions

The asymmetry is critical: generating synthetic content costs pennies; verification requires substantial human expertise.

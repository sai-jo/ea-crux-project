## The Scenario

By 2040, most of humanity's questions are answered by 2-3 dominant AI systems. Search engines are replaced. Libraries are bypassed. Experts are consulted less. The AI becomes the default—often only—interface between humans and knowledge.

**The risks:**
- If AI is wrong, everyone is wrong together
- If AI is biased, all knowledge is biased the same way
- If AI is captured (by governments, corporations, ideologies), knowledge is captured
- If AI systems fail, no backup exists

This isn't hypothetical concentration—it's the natural trajectory of current AI development.

## How Monopoly Forms

### Phase 1: Competition (2020-2025)
- Multiple AI companies compete
- Open source alternatives viable
- Users have choice
- Different AIs give different answers

### Phase 2: Consolidation (2025-2030)
- Training costs exclude smaller players
- Open source falls behind frontier
- 3-5 major providers remain
- Similar training data → similar outputs

### Phase 3: Concentration (2030-2040)
- 2-3 AI systems dominate
- Most queries go through same systems
- Alternatives exist but marginal
- AI becomes default knowledge interface

### Phase 4: Monopoly (2035-2050?)
- Single AI paradigm dominates
- All knowledge accessed through one interface
- No meaningful alternatives
- AI-generated content trains future AI (feedback loop)

## Failure Modes

### Correlated Error

| Problem | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Shared training data** | All AIs trained on similar data → similar errors |
| **Architectural monoculture** | Same architectures → same failure modes |
| **No independent verification** | Can't cross-check with different source |
| **Hallucinations** | Same hallucinations across systems |

**Example**: If all AI systems are trained on the internet, and the internet contains systematic biases, all AI systems inherit those biases together.

### Knowledge Capture

| Threat | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Corporate control** | AI optimizes for business interests |
| **Government influence** | States pressure AI companies |
| **Ideological bias** | Training choices embed worldview |
| **Commercial distortion** | Knowledge shaped for monetization |

### Single Point of Failure

| Failure Type | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Technical outage** | Billions lose knowledge access |
| **Cyberattack** | Knowledge infrastructure compromised |
| **Bankruptcy** | AI company fails; knowledge lost |
| **Regulatory shutdown** | Government closes provider |

### Epistemic Lock-In

| Problem | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **AI trains on AI output** | Feedback loop amplifies errors |
| **Human expertise atrophies** | Can't verify AI answers |
| **Alternative knowledge dies** | Non-AI sources abandoned |
| **Errors become "truth"** | Repeated across all queries |

## Domains at Risk

### Education

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Curriculum standardization** | AI answers become "official" knowledge |
| **Critical thinking decline** | Why question if AI knows? |
| **Teacher obsolescence** | AI tutors replace human instruction |
| **Testing homogenization** | AI-graded, AI-studied, AI-taught |

### Science

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Research direction controlled** | AI suggests what to study |
| **Literature access** | AI summarizes; originals not read |
| **Hypothesis generation** | AI generates hypotheses |
| **Peer review** | AI reviews AI-assisted papers |

### Medicine

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Diagnosis monopoly** | All doctors use same AI |
| **Treatment standardization** | AI recommends same treatments |
| **Medical knowledge** | AI becomes medical authority |
| **Errors scale** | Wrong AI diagnosis → millions affected |

### Law

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Legal research** | AI becomes source of precedent |
| **Contract generation** | AI writes all contracts |
| **Legal interpretation** | AI determines what law means |
| **Judicial assistance** | Judges rely on AI analysis |

### Public Discourse

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Fact-checking monopoly** | AI determines what's true |
| **Information access** | AI filters all information |
| **Historical narrative** | AI tells what happened |
| **Public opinion** | AI shapes by answering queries |

## Defenses

### Technical

| Approach | Description | Challenge |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Open source AI** | Maintain non-monopoly alternatives | Falling behind frontier |
| **Federated training** | Distribute AI development | Coordination problem |
| **Diverse architectures** | Different AI approaches | Efficiency pressure toward monoculture |
| **Local AI** | AI on personal devices | Capability limited |

### Regulatory

| Approach | Description | Challenge |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Antitrust enforcement** | Break up AI monopolies | Jurisdictional limits |
| **Interoperability mandates** | Prevent lock-in | Technical complexity |
| **Public AI options** | Government-funded alternatives | Political challenges |
| **Data commons** | Shared training data | IP concerns |

### Institutional

| Approach | Description | Challenge |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Library independence** | Maintain non-AI knowledge access | Funding |
| **Academic preservation** | Universities as knowledge alternatives | Incentive pressure |
| **Journalism investment** | Human-verified information | Economic model broken |
| **Expert maintenance** | Preserve human expertise | Expertise atrophy |

### Structural

| Approach | Description | Challenge |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Knowledge sovereignty** | National/regional AI alternatives | Capability gaps |
| **Analog preservation** | Non-digital knowledge backup | Access difficulty |
| **Multi-source mandates** | Require diverse knowledge sources | Enforcement |

## Overview

Reality fragmentation represents the breakdown of shared epistemological foundations in society, where different populations operate with **incompatible beliefs about basic facts**. This goes beyond political polarization—people don't just disagree about policies, but about what is actually happening in the world.

> **For comprehensive analysis**, see [Reality Coherence](/knowledge-base/parameters/reality-coherence/), which covers:
> - Current coherence levels across populations and domains
> - AI-driven fragmentation mechanisms (personalization, synthetic content)
> - Factors that increase and decrease coherence
> - Intervention effectiveness and research directions
> - Trajectory scenarios through 2030

## Current Evidence

### Documented Belief Divergences

| Domain | Group A Belief | Group B Belief | Split |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-------|
| **COVID-19 Deaths** | 1M+ Americans died | Deaths overcounted by 50%+ | 78% vs 22% |
| **2020 Election** | Biden won legitimately | Election was stolen | 61% vs 39% |
| **Climate Data** | Human-caused warming | Natural cycles/hoax | 71% vs 29% |

*Source: <R id="c67537d289bb7a7e">Pew Research</R>, <R id="b63a8ecfadae3006">Gallup</R>*

### Information Environment Isolation

| Metric | 2010 | 2024 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Cross-partisan news overlap | 47% | 12% | -35% |
| Trust in "news media" | 54% | 31% | -23% |
| Social media as primary news | 23% | 67% | +44% |

## AI Acceleration Mechanisms

| AI Capability | Fragmentation Effect |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Algorithmic curation** | Each user sees different reality |
| **Synthetic content** | Infinite supporting "evidence" for any worldview |
| **Deepfake generation** | Fabricated audiovisual "proof" |
| **Micro-targeting** | Psychologically tailored messaging |

The feedback loop: User belief → AI recommends confirming content → Engagement increases → Algorithm learns preference → More extreme content recommended → Stronger belief formation → Repeat.

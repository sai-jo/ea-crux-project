## What Is AI-Assisted Deliberation?

AI-assisted deliberation uses AI to help large groups of people:
- **Discuss** complex issues productively
- **Find common ground** across divides
- **Generate proposals** that represent genuine consensus
- **Scale** deliberation beyond small groups

Unlike voting (which aggregates existing preferences) or polling (which measures opinions), deliberation **changes minds through dialogue**.

---

## Why This Matters

### The Democracy Problem

| Challenge | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scale limits** | Can't have productive conversations with millions |
| **Polarization** | Public discourse amplifies disagreement |
| **Elite capture** | Small groups dominate policy |
| **Legitimacy crisis** | Citizens don't feel heard |
| **Complexity** | Issues too complex for simple votes |

### The AI Opportunity

| AI Capability | Application |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Summarization** | Distill thousands of comments |
| **Clustering** | Find hidden areas of agreement |
| **Translation** | Bridge language and framing barriers |
| **Facilitation** | Guide productive conversation |
| **Synthesis** | Generate proposals from discussions |

## Current Platforms and Experiments

### Polis

**What**: Open-source platform for large-scale opinion gathering and consensus-finding.

**How it works**:
1. Participants submit short statements
2. Others vote agree/disagree/pass
3. AI clusters participants by opinion similarity
4. Platform identifies statements with cross-group consensus
5. Visualization shows opinion landscape

**Notable uses**:
- **vTaiwan**: Uber regulation in Taiwan (thousands participated)
- **Taiwan COVID response**: Citizen input on policies
- **Various city/national consultations**

**Strengths**:
- Scales to thousands
- Finds hidden consensus
- Reduces polarization dynamics
- Open source

**Links**:
- [Polis platform](https://pol.is/)
- [Computational Democracy Project](https://compdemocracy.org/)

### Collective Constitutional AI (Anthropic)

**What**: Using collective input to shape AI behavior.

**How it works**:
1. Large group provides feedback on AI behavior
2. AI trained on aggregated preferences
3. Constitution reflects collective input

**Experiment**:
- 1000+ participants across political spectrum
- Deliberated on what AI should do in tricky situations
- Reached surprising consensus on many issues

**Research**: [Anthropic: Collective Constitutional AI](https://www.anthropic.com/news/collective-constitutional-ai-aligning-a-language-model-with-public-input)

### All Our Ideas

**What**: Wiki surveys that scale open-ended input.

**How it works**:
1. Participants compare pairs of ideas
2. Participants can add new ideas
3. System builds preference ranking
4. New ideas compete with existing ones

**Uses**:
- NYC participatory budgeting
- Corporate strategy input
- Research prioritization

**Link**: [All Our Ideas](https://www.allourideas.org/)

### Deliberative Polling

**What**: Traditional deliberation combined with polling (Fishkin method).

**How it works**:
1. Random sample of citizens selected
2. Given balanced briefing materials
3. Small-group facilitated discussions
4. Pre/post polling measures opinion change
5. Results inform policy

**AI enhancement**: AI can help with briefing materials, facilitation, analysis.

**Link**: [Stanford Deliberative Democracy Lab](https://deliberation.stanford.edu/)

### AI Town Halls (Emerging)

**What**: AI-facilitated real-time large group discussions.

**How it works**:
1. Participants join synchronous session
2. AI moderates, summarizes, finds patterns
3. Real-time synthesis of emerging consensus
4. Structured output for decision-makers

**Status**: Experimental; limited deployment.

## Design Principles

### 1. Genuine Deliberation, Not Just Aggregation

| Wrong Approach | Right Approach |
|----------------|----------------|
| Vote on fixed options | Generate options through dialogue |
| Measure existing opinions | Enable opinion change |
| Average preferences | Find reasoned consensus |

### 2. Representation and Access

| Requirement | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| Random sampling | Representative participation |
| Multiple formats | Accommodate different communication styles |
| Accessibility | Remove barriers to participation |
| Time for reflection | Not just rapid-fire |

### 3. Transparency

| Requirement | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| Clear methodology | Explain how AI processes input |
| Visible clusters | Show how opinions are grouped |
| Audit trail | Track how synthesis was generated |

### 4. Connection to Power

| Challenge | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| Deliberation without impact | Formal connection to decision-makers |
| Decision-makers ignore input | Pre-commitment to respond |
| Outcomes don't reflect deliberation | Track follow-through |

## Challenges

### Technical

| Challenge | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Summarization fidelity** | AI may distort or miss nuance |
| **Gaming** | Bad actors may try to manipulate |
| **Bias in clustering** | AI choices affect outcomes |
| **Scale vs depth** | Harder to deliberate deeply at scale |

### Political

| Challenge | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Legitimacy** | Do participants represent the public? |
| **Binding power** | Will decision-makers respect outcomes? |
| **Capture** | Who controls the platform? |
| **Competing with existing processes** | How does this interact with elections, etc? |

### Behavioral

| Challenge | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Participation burden** | Deliberation takes time and effort |
| **Self-selection** | Who chooses to participate? |
| **Motivated reasoning** | People may not be open to changing minds |
| **Facilitation quality** | AI facilitation may not be good enough |
